Looking Back on Project Work 1

Published: May 8, 2026

Hi everyone, Oleg here. The first semester is done, and I want to share a few thoughts on my first project: Developing Message Architecture for the Personal Brand. It was a solo project about building a clear communication strategy for my own freelance work in programmatic advertising - and honestly, it turned out to be one of the most useful things I've done so far at FH Joanneum.

What worked

The best part was the research.I picked five freelance profiles on Upwork and LinkedIn and scored them across seven criteria. Sounds dry, but it gave me a real picture of the market instead of just a gut feeling. More importantly, it forced me to be honest about my own positioning. I could see where my agency background actually helps me stand out, and where I was just hiding behind jargon that no real client would care about.

A big part of this also came from working with my supervisor, Doris Eichmeier. We had regular check-ins, and the first presentation in particular gave me a lot of ideas that I ended up using in the final architecture. Without those conversations, the project would have been much weaker.

What I'd change

The hardest part was time. I spent way more hours on this than I planned, and I kept jumping between different sections of the paper instead of finishing one before starting the next. A lot of that time was just rework. For Project Work 2, I want to plan things in a proper order and finish each part before moving on. Simple, but I clearly didn't do it this time.

The bigger lesson

The most interesting thing happened after the project was done. I started freelancing alongside my studies, and the message architecture I built for a school assignment is now actually helping me talk to clients. It's doing exactly what it was supposed to do.

So the main thing I took from this project is pretty simple: content strategy isn't only for big companies. If you don't know what you're doing with it, it just becomes more corporate noise. But if you use it well, it works just as much for one person as it does for a big brand. Even a one-person business needs a clear message.

I'm still figuring out what Project Work 2 will be about. But this first one set the bar - research first, honest thinking second, and then writing in a way that real people, not just classmates and professors, would actually want to read.
