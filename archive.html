Where the Lecture Hall Meets the Ad Account

Published: July 5, 2026

Hi again! Some people ask me why I do a master's degree when I already work in this field. I freelance and run ad campaigns on Meta and DV360. My honest answer is: COS is not just theory for me. I see it again in my actual campaigns.

One example is audience segmentation. In our lectures we talk about this with words like personas, needs, motivations. When I build a campaign in DV360, I do almost the same thing, just with different names. I choose in-market and affinity segments, and then I write ads that match how each group thinks, not only their age or location. Before COS, I would just say "target group: women 25-34" to a client. Now I try to explain it in normal words, not just platform terms like "1st-party audience overlap". Clients understand it better this way.

Another example is message architecture. For Project Work 1, I built a message architecture for my own freelance profile. Now I use a small version of the same method before I write ads for a client. I ask myself: what is the one thing this brand wants a stranger to believe? And then I check if the headline, the banner and the DV360 placement all support that one message. Before COS, I just started writing headlines directly, without this step.

My Meta and DV360 dashboards look the same as before. But the campaigns I build are a bit different now - less generic headlines, and I can explain my decisions better to clients. It's a strange kind of result from a master's program: it doesn't show in a grade, it shows in a client meeting.
